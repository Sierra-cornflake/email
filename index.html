Tuesday, August 16, was designated by the U.S. Election Assistance Commission (EAC) as National Poll Worker Recruitment Day, a day of action with the goal of encouraging potential poll workers to sign up to Help America Vote in communities across the country. 

 

Snohomish County hasn’t used poll workers since 2006 when our elections moved entirely to vote by mail. Instead, we hire seasonal election workers like you who work to verify signatures, close drop boxes, process ballots, and assist with registering voters.

 

You are essential to our mission of conducting fair, accountable elections and encouraging people to understand and participate in the voting process and we cannot thank you enough.

Serving as a seasonal election worker is a valuable way to give back to your community and ensure safe, free, and fair elections for all voters. We appreciate all your hard work and are very grateful to all of you who return year after year to help with elections.
